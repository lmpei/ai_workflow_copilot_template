# Stage E Plan

## Stage Name

`Stage E: Support and Job Workbench Productization`

## Status

- Status: active
- Opened At: 2026-03-27
- First Task Wave: active

## Position In The Project

Stage E begins after the formal closeout of Stage D. Stage D proved that the shared platform can now be reached through
one real public internet demo path with bounded operator discipline. Stage E narrows the next bounded step to one
product-depth outcome: turn the non-Research scenario modules from task-result workflows into persistent workbench
surfaces without breaking the live public demo baseline.

## Planning Model

Stage E operates under the same three-track roadmap model:

1. `Research`
2. `Platform Reliability`
3. `Delivery and Operations`

The tracks remain parallel, but Stage E changes the emphasis again. Research remains the reference workbench pattern,
while the concrete product work now shifts into Support and Job persistent-object depth.

## Priority Model

- Primary track:
  - `Platform Reliability` as the boundary-setting layer for persistent non-Research work objects
- Required parallel tracks:
  - `Research` as the reference workbench and workflow depth model
  - `Delivery and Operations` to keep the live public demo stable while new workbench state is introduced

Stage E should not optimize for new AI capability waves if those waves delay Support and Job workbench depth or make the
live demo harder to operate.

## Stage Goal

Turn Support Copilot and Job Assistant from structured task-result workflows into durable workbench surfaces with
persistent work objects, operator-readable state, and reusable history, while preserving the live public internet demo
baseline reached in Stage D.

## Track 1: Research

### Objective

Keep Research as the reference workbench so Stage E reuses the right persistence and workflow lessons instead of
blindly copying implementation details.

### Focus Areas

- preserve Research as the deepest reference workflow during Stage E
- reuse only the durable ideas that fit Support and Job instead of cloning the Research asset layer mechanically
- keep one grounded module path visible in the live public demo while Stage E expands non-Research depth

### Expected Outcomes

- Stage E workbench layers feel consistent with the existing platform instead of becoming special-case silos
- the public demo still has one clearly understandable reference workflow even while Support and Job deepen

## Track 2: Platform Reliability

### Objective

Define safe persistent-object boundaries for Support and Job without undermining the shared runtime or operator
controls.

### Focus Areas

- work-object persistence contracts for Support cases and Job hiring packets or shortlists
- ownership, status, timeline, and history boundaries for those work objects
- relationship rules between tasks, follow-ups, comparisons, and persistent workbench state
- operator-visible recovery and continuity expectations when workbench state is refreshed or migrated

### Expected Outcomes

- Support and Job no longer rely on raw task history as the only durable collaboration surface
- persistent state remains explicit and inspectable instead of leaking through ad hoc JSON or hidden coupling

## Track 3: Delivery and Operations

### Objective

Keep the live public demo usable while Stage E introduces deeper stateful workbench behavior.

### Focus Areas

- public-demo continuity while new workbench objects are added
- demo-safe refresh guidance when persistent non-Research state exists
- bounded operator expectations for keeping showcase paths understandable after Stage E changes
- honest public-facing communication of what is stable, what is demo-grade, and what may be reset between demos

### Expected Outcomes

- the Stage D live demo remains usable during Stage E
- future demos and interviews can show workbench depth without hidden cleanup or improvised reset behavior

## Non-Goals

Stage E does not primarily optimize for:

- new MCP, realtime, or computer-use capability waves
- enterprise auth, billing, or organization management
- production-grade multi-tenant SaaS hardening
- a full CRM, ticketing system, or applicant-tracking system
- renaming the three scenario modules

## Execution Rules

- Stage E tasks should use `stage-e-*` naming
- Stage E should keep Support and Job workbench scope bounded and should not drift into broad product-suite ambition
- public-demo continuity should stay honest about demo limits rather than implying stronger operational maturity than exists
- Stage E should prefer durable state clarity over UI breadth

## Success Criteria

Stage E is successful when:

- Support Copilot has a persistent case workbench with durable case state, timeline, and reusable case packet behavior
- Job Assistant has a persistent hiring workbench with durable shortlist or hiring-packet state and comparison history
- the live public demo remains usable and understandable after those workbench layers are introduced
- the operator has a bounded refresh and continuity story for the new workbench state
