# Task: stage-f-11-showcase-visual-polish

## Goal

Apply one bounded showcase-quality visual pass after the new site and workbench structure is in place.

## Project Stage

- Stage: Stage F
- Track: Delivery and Operations with Research and Platform Reliability support

## Why

Visual polish still matters, but only after the product shape is right. This task exists to make the highest-traffic
surfaces feel intentional and presentable once the personal-homepage split, workspace-center reset, and chat-first
workbench model have already landed.

## Context

Relevant documents:

- `docs/prd/STAGE_F_PLAN.md`
- `tasks/stage-f-10-supporting-panels-and-analytics-drawer.md`
- `STATUS.md`

## Scope

Allowed work:

- improve layout hierarchy, spacing, typography, and visual emphasis on the highest-traffic pages
- remove visible presentation defects that damage trust
- reinforce the new hierarchy rather than adding decorative noise

Disallowed work:

- a full design-system rewrite across every page in the repository
- purely decorative motion or styling that does not improve comprehension
- backend or deployment changes unrelated to the user-facing visual surface

## Acceptance Criteria

- the most important public-facing and project-facing pages feel materially more intentional and presentable
- visual hierarchy reinforces the new product structure instead of fighting it
- Stage F remains bounded and does not turn into an endless cosmetic polish track
